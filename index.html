JOHN BOELEE

Early work: Looking Back (1993)

An early charcoal drawing by Dutch contemporary artist John Boelee, made in 1993 at Diergaarde Blijdorp in Rotterdam. As one of the artist's earliest surviving works, it marks the beginning of a practice concerned with how image and form emerge through observation — a concern that continues through his later work in abstraction and mixed media.

Looking Back (1993)
Charcoal on paper
40 × 57 cm
Drawn at Diergaarde Blijdorp, Rotterdam

Recent work: Layers of Absence (2025)

"Layers of Absence" (2025) is a mixed media work by John Boelee, combining ink and acrylic paint on paper with reverse-painted glass. Working between abstraction and representation, the piece builds up and obscures layers of image and material, creating a tension between presence and absence — between what is revealed and what remains unresolved.

LAYERS OF ABSENCE (2025)
Ink and acrylic paint on paper and reverse-painted glass
59 × 49 cm

Recent work: Untitled (2025)

An untitled mixed media work from 2025, made with ink and acrylic paint on paper and reverse-painted glass. Companion to "Layers of Absence," it continues the artist's investigation of image, perception and material — layers of gesture and fragment held between what is shown and what stays withheld.

Untitled (2025)
Ink and acrylic paint on paper and reverse-painted glass
59 × 49 cm

Studio practice: Emotional Disturbance #9 (2021)

"Emotional Disturbance #9" (2021) is a large-scale, airbrushed acrylic painting on canvas by John Boelee, measuring 180 by 180 centimetres. Photographed in the artist's studio, the work sits within his ongoing practice between abstraction and representation, using material and gesture to explore how an image emerges and holds together at scale.

Biography

Born in Leiden, the Netherlands; based in Spain

Royal Academy of Art, The Hague (1992–1996)

Selected group exhibitions and art fairs include Pulchri Studio (The Hague), Gallery Delta (Rotterdam), ART RAI (Amsterdam, with Gallery Delta), Affordable Art Fair (with ARTITLEDcontemporary), and Chinese Whispers, a two-location exhibition presented at Gallery Nasty Alice (Eindhoven, the Netherlands) and Karin Janssen Project Space (London, UK).

Solo exhibitions include Gallery Delta (Rotterdam), and Pulchri Studio (The Hague).
